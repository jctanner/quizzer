Use the laws of propositional logic to prove the following:

(a) ¬p → ¬q ≡ q → p

(b) p ∧ (¬p → q) ≡ p

(c) (p → q) ∧ (p → r) ≡ p → (q ∧ r)

(d) ¬p → (q → r) ≡ q → (p ∨ r)


(e) (p → r) ∨ (q → r) ≡ (p ∧ q) → r


(f) ¬(p ∨ (¬p ∧ q)) ≡ ¬p ∧ ¬q


(g) (p ∧ q ∧ ¬r) ∨ (p ∧ ¬q ∧ ¬r) ≡ p ∧ ¬r


(h) p ↔ (p ∧ r) ≡ ¬p ∨ r
